A stoppage-time equalizer forced extra time before a late header settled the tie in front of a capacity crowd at a packed stadium on Tuesday night.

The holders looked set for an early exit after falling behind in the second half, but a well-worked set piece in the fifth minute of added time leveled the score and sent the match into an additional thirty minutes.

With fatigue setting in on both sides, it was a substitute who made the difference, rising above two defenders to head home the winner in the 109th minute. The result sends the defending champions through to next month's final, where they will face the winner of Wednesday's second semi-final.

"We never stopped believing," the match-winning substitute said afterward. "The crowd carried us through when our legs were gone."

The final is scheduled to be played at a neutral venue, with ticket sales expected to open within the coming days.
