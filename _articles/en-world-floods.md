Aid agencies report that the first supply routes reopened Thursday after days of heavy rainfall left several coastal towns cut off from the mainland.

Relief convoys carrying food, clean water and medical supplies began reaching the hardest-hit districts overnight, following emergency repairs to a key bridge that had been washed out earlier in the week.

Local officials say more than ten thousand residents were affected by the flooding, with several hundred still sheltering in temporary evacuation centers. Weather services say conditions are expected to improve over the coming days, though a second system remains under watch further offshore.

International aid organizations have pledged additional support as recovery efforts continue.
