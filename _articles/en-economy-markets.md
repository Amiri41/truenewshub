Major indices closed sharply higher on Monday after fresh consumer price data showed inflation slowing more than analysts had forecast, easing pressure on policymakers ahead of this month's rate decision.

The benchmark index gained close to two percent by the closing bell, its strongest single-day performance in several months. Technology and consumer discretionary shares led the advance, while defensive sectors lagged behind.

Analysts said the figures reinforce expectations that the central bank may hold rates steady at its next meeting, a shift from earlier forecasts that had priced in a further increase.

"This is the clearest signal yet that the tightening cycle may be nearing its end," one market strategist said in a note to clients.
